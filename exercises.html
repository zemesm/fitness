Form of Exercises

Upper Body

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

Mainly targets Shoulders, secondarily triceps.

  • Sit down with your weight on the bench set at 75 degree angle.
  • Lock your shoulders and bend your back slightly as on the bench press.
  • Lock your shoulders and bend your back slightly as on the bench press.
  • Kick your weight up with your legs if needed.
  • Continue by pressing the weight up to your strainght arms and down so that your elbows pass your chin slightly.



Standing Twisting Dumbbell Curls

Targets biceps.

  • Take your weight, stand up and curl the weight from extended arms to the flexed position of your biceps.
  • When doing the eccentric portion of the movement twist to the supinated position and when doing the concentric part of the movement twist to the neutral grip position.
  • Try to fixate your elbows as much as possible and dont move them.
  • Always comes to the bottom of the movement where your biceps gets lenghtened.
  • Dont swing, do repetitions strictly, slight swing are allowed in the last few reps.

Overhead Triceps Extensions

Targets triceps (mostly long head).

  • Stand up with your dumbbell, pick it up with your hands from the sides at the top as in the video and start in the top position.
  • From there lower the weight until your elbows move slightly past the 90 degree angle.
  • Try to not allow your elbows to move outward, but keep them straight.





Seated Dumbbell Lateral Raises

Targets shoulders (lateral head).

  • Sit down on a bench with your pair of dumbbells to your sides.
  • When doing this movement bend slightly forward with your back straight.
  • Bend your arms slightly, move up focusing on keeping your arms fixated and on pulling the weight up through your elbow, not through your arms.
  • On the top of the concentric part of the movement your arms from your shoulders to elbows should be perfectly aligned with the ground, but your arms from the elbows to your forearms will be facing down at an 10-20 percent angle.
  • Try not to move your body as much as possibly so that you target shoulders more without using momentum.
  • When going up with your arms bent, do not try to go perfectly on the sides, your arms will be facing slightly forward as well.

Barebell Biceps Curls

Targets biceps.

  • Take your weight, stand up and curl the weight from extended arms to the flexed position of your biceps in front of your body.
  • Try to fixate your elbows as much as possible and dont move them.
  • Always comes to the bottom of the movement where your biceps gets lenghtened.
  • Dont swing, do repetitions strictly, slight swing are allowed in the last few reps.









Barebell Bench Press

Mainly targets chest, secondly targets shoulders and triceps.

  • Lay down on a bench, fixate your shoulderblades back and down and from now on do not move your shoulders at all, bend your back slightly and set up your legs to the angle that is the msot comfortable for you.
  • Take the barbell so that your forearms are in a 90 degree angle with the ground on the bottom of the movement.
  • Lower the weight to under your chest (always touch your chest with the weight) with your elbows facing down at an angle of 15-30 degrees and then push it back up.
  • This movement pattern is not a straight line, as you are lowering the weight from straight arms the weight will move from on top of your neck to the top of your lower chest if bending arms properly.
  • Remember to keep your grip straight and dont let it just fall off and relax.



Dumbbell Rows

Mainly targets lats, but also other parts of your back.

  • When starting with your right hand, move to the left side of the bench, place your left hand on the front of the bench and your left bent leg in the middle of the bench.
  • Pick up the dumbbell from the ground and make your back perfectly straight.
  • Start in a neutral or pronated position and as you move the weight up translate to neutral or supinated position gradually for a better feeling of your lats.
  • When rowing the weight up, try to pull it through your elbow to your hip.
  • On the top of the movement, your arms should be almost in a 90 degree angle and on the bottom your arm should be perfectly straight to stretch the lat.
  • After finishing one arm switch to the other, move to the other part of the bench and switch your leg and arms wtich the exact same positions on the bench.



Dumbbell Bench Press

Mainly targets your chest, secondly your shoulders and triceps.

  • Take the dumbbells, place them on your quads and kick each dumbbell with your leg so that you can lay down and get into the position.
  • Lay down on a bench, fixate your shoulderblades back and down and from now on do not move your shoulders at all, bend your back slightly and set up your legs to the angle that is the msot comfortable for you.
  • Lower the weight to under your chest with your elbows facing down at an angle of 15-30 degrees while focusing to pass your shoulders with your elbows on the bottom part of the movement and then push it back up.
  • This movement pattern is not a straight line, as you are lowering the weight from straight arms the weight will move from on top of your neck to the top of your lower chest if bending arms properly.
  • Remember to keep your grip straight and dont let it just fall off and relax.

Wide Pronated Grip Lat Pulldown

Depending on how you do the movement mainly targets lats, secondly your upper back and other parts of your back or.

  • Take the weight, bend your back slightly as if you are doing the bench press and grip the bar as widely as comfortable for you.
  • When pulling the weight you can lean back slightly (but the more you lean, the more you target upper back and the less you target your lats).
  • Always pull the bar to your upper chest, reaching minimally your chin, maximally touching your upper chest, but never past that.









High Cable Flies

Targets chest.

  • Set up the cables with the attachement (or a simmilar one) as I am using in the video.
  • Go 2 meters ahead of the machine with the weight in your arms, lean forward, set up your legs as in the video and start the movement.
  • At the top position go slightly higher with your elbow than your shoulder is for an ideal stretch of your chest and touoch your hands at the bottom of the movement.
  • Keep your arms slightly bent during the movement and always fixate them so they do not change the angle that you choose.
  • Perform the movement as if you wanted to hug someone or as if you wanted to touch your fists, this will help you with the optimal flex of the targetted muscle.

Rope Lat Pullover

Targets lats.

  • Add the rope attachment to the machine, take the rope, lean forward and put one leg in front of the other like in the video..
  • Always keep your arms straight or very slightly bent during this movement and do not change the angle while in the middle of the set.
  • Pull the rope in a 1/3 circle to your waist and stretch at the top of the movement by letting your arms higher than your shoulders.







Dumbbell Hammer Curls

Targets biceps.

  • Take your weight, stand up and curl the weight from extended arms to the flexed position of your biceps in front of your body touching your chest.
  • Try to fixate your elbows as much as possible and dont move them.
  • Always comes to the bottom of the movement where your biceps gets lenghtened at the sides of your body.
  • Dont swing, do repetitions strictly, slight swing are allowed in the last few reps.
  • This movement can be also done by not curling the weight in front of you touching your chest, but curling at the sides with both arm simoultanesly directly in line with your arms.

Straight Bar Skullcrushers

Targets triceps (mainly medial head).

  • Lay down on a bench with the bar laying on your lap and push it up just as doing bench press (the laying set up is exactly the same).
  • Push the weight up to your straight arms, and then lean your arms slightly backwards to 10-20 degree angle (this allows your triceps to be always flexed).
  • Lower the weight to your forehead almost touching it while keeping your elbows fixed at all times.

Weighted Pull Ups

Targets Lats and Back.

  • Take the belt and add the amount of weight that you can handle.
  • When jumping on the bar, always lock the weight between your legs, otherwise it is going to hurt in places that you do not want it to hurt.
  • Pull yourself up ideally to your chin or chest, or later when you are more faligued to your nose or forehead.
  • Do not swing the weight, because that will defeat the purpose of adding weight by using more momentum.








Barebell Row

Mainly targets lats or upper back depending on how you do the movement and lower back, but also other parts of your back.

  • Take the barebell, bend forward as much as you can almost to a 90 degree angle and slightly bend your legs.
  • Row t he weight from bottom to the top with your back straight at all times witkout using much momentum.
  • When rowing the weight up, try to pull it through your elbow to your hip.
  • On the top of the movement, your arms should be almost in a 90 degree angle and on the bottom your arm should be perfectly straight to stretch the lat.

Machine Chest Press

Targets chest.

  • Sit down, choose the weight you want to lift and adjust the height of your seat.
  • Get into the position by sliding into the machine from the top.
  • Push the weight to your straight arms or close to it and then lower the weight for your elbows to pass your shoulders.
  • Go slowly and stop at the bottom of the movement to feel the muscle more.







Dumbbell Lat Pullover

Targets lats.

  • Lay on the bench with your back slightly bent.
  • Push the weight form your chest to your straight arms and then lower it behind your head with your arms straight.
  • You can have your arms slightly bent or fully straight but keep them at the same angle throughout the movement.
  • Imagine the movement pattern as a 1/4 of a circle.

Lower Body

Dumbbell Bulgarian Split Squats

Targets quadriceps, hamstrings and glutes.

  • Set up your bench on flat.
  • Take your dumbbells and put on foot behind you and the bench.
  • Set up your legs so that your knee does not go farther on the bottom of the movement than your tip is.
  • When going to the bottom always try to move your leg to the 90 degree angle or try to go almost to the ground with your other leg because the lower you are the more your glutes work.

    • To put more weight on your leg lean slightly forward with your back straight when doing this movement.

  • After finishing with your one leg switch to the other and do the same amount of repetitions.




Quad Extensions

Targets quadriceps.

  • Firstly adjust your machine to your preferences (the distance of the seat, then the height of the pas where your heels are).
  • Then make sure you press the button and put the pad up so you can sit to the seat.
  • Then put how much weight you want to lift and adjust the pad to the lowest level possible so that you dont slam the weight when going to the bottom part of the movement.
  • Starting form the bottom position flex your quads going to the top making sure your legs are always completely extended at the top, becase there is the most tension on the targeted muscle.
  • For more added intensity you can stop this movement for a while at the top of the movement for a better feel.
  • When going down try to reach the bottom of the movement because there the muscle lenghtens.

Dumbbell Romanian Deadlifts

Mainly targets hamstrings, secondly glutes.

  • Stand up with your dumbbells, lean backwards with your lower body and forwards with your upper body while keeping your back straight at all times and your shoulderblader retracted.
  • Imitate wanting to touch your toes with the dumbbells, except your do not keep your hamstrings completely straight, but you bend your legs slighty.
  • When struggling with keeping your back straight imagine trying to stick out your butt outwards.
  • During your set keep your head to the ground ideally at all times, but it is okay to check your form in mirror occasionally.





Machine Calf Raises

Targets calves.

  • Set up the weight on your machine, sit down and adjust the height of the pads.
  • Lift the weight with your calves and remove the fuse.
  • Stretch your calves as much as possible at the bottom of the movement and flex your calves as much as you can at the top.
  • You can go slowly and even stop at the bottom and at the top of the movement for a better mind muscle connection.
  • Never pull the machine with your arms, because this will redirect the weight to your arms rather that to your calves (for this purpose I keep my arms under the seat).
